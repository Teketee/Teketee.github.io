Homicides in Mexico

The endless War on Drugs

Posted on March 9th, 2021

December 11th, 2006 is known as the official date of the start of Mexico's long war on Drugs with Operation Michoac√°n. From 2006 to 2018, there were an estimated 150,000+ homicides related to organized crime. Unfortunately many innocent lives have been caught in the middle, including some related to me. While it seems to only be getting worse, I started this project as a foot in the door to spread awarness for those unable to and hope to someday help be apart of the change.

In this project, I grabbed some data sets from the National Institute of Statistics and Geography which had the deaths for women, men, and both for each state in Mexico from 2010 - 2019. I used python libraries such as Matplotlib and Pandas to calculate the homicide rate per year, homicide yearly percent change, and homicides per 100,00 in each state from 2017 - 2019.
